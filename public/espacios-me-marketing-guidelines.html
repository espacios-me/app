Espacios.me Marketing Guidelines

This page defines how each public page should look, feel, and communicate. It keeps design language consistent with espacios.me: high-contrast hero, minimal typography, muted supporting text, and clear conversion intent.

Global design rules

Apply these to every marketing page before writing page-specific content.

Visual language

Keep the same premium monochrome identity used on espacios.me.

  • Dark hero (#0D0D0D) with soft white text.
  • Light content sections with thin borders and generous spacing.
  • Rounded cards (12–14px) and subtle dividers.

Typography and hierarchy

Write with short, direct lines and intentional contrast.

  • One bold, high-impact H1.
  • Use small uppercase labels for section context.
  • Body text should stay muted and readable (14–17px).

Call-to-action structure

Every page needs one primary action and one secondary fallback.

  • Primary CTA: solid/high contrast button.
  • Secondary CTA: ghost or text link.
  • Place CTA above the fold and at section endings.

Messaging tone

Speak like an operator, not a hype marketer.

  • Lead with outcomes and conversion intent.
  • Avoid exaggerated promises.
  • Show process clarity in short bullet lists.

Page-by-page layout guidelines

Recommended content skeleton for each existing route.

Home page (/)

  • Hero: clear positioning statement + proof stats.
  • Services grid: lead generation, AI optimization, nurturing.
  • Calculator or ROI section for conversion framing.
  • End with a direct consult CTA.

Atom landing (/atom)

  • Product-first hero and concise feature breakdown.
  • Focus on memory, context, reminders, and privacy.
  • Use waitlist CTA as the single main conversion path.
  • Include trust notes on data control.

Privacy pages (/privacy, /atom/privacy)

  • Simple legal layout with readable line-length.
  • Timestamp the policy update date.
  • Section blocks: data collected, usage, retention, contact.
  • No heavy visuals; prioritize clarity.

Terms pages (/terms, /atom/terms)

  • Same legal template style as privacy pages.
  • Define scope, user responsibilities, limitations, termination.
  • Keep headings scannable and numbered when possible.
  • End with support contact details.
